## PART 1 — PHILOSOPHY

### What a Report Is

A report is not a text file. A report is not a markdown dump. A report is a **premium intelligence product** — a living, visual, branded document hosted on appstarfamily.com/reports that combines deep research with beautiful presentation. It should look like something a $500/hour consultant would deliver. It should feel like the Pruttius Empire takes its intelligence seriously. Because it does.

### What a Report Is Not

- Not a wall of text
- Not a list of bullet points without context
- Not vague qualitative labels (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW) without real data
- Not a PDF that sits in a folder nobody opens
- Not a one-time document — reports can be updated as markets evolve

### The Reader

Every report is written for JJ — who is usually on a beach, thinks fast, and needs to make decisions quickly. That means:

- Lead with the most important finding, not the methodology
- Make the data visual — he should be able to scan the infographics and understand the situation in 60 seconds
- Use prose to add context and nuance that the visuals can't carry
- Never bury the action items

## PART 2 — RESEARCH STANDARDS

### The Non-Negotiable Data Rules

**Rule 1: Real numbers only.**
Every keyword must have an estimated monthly search volume as a number (e.g. 12,400/mo) not a label (e.g. HIGH). Volume estimates must be derived from SERP density analysis, cross-referenced with industry CPC benchmarks, with confidence levels stated. Vague labels are not acceptable in any table that will appear in a published report.

**Rule 2: Real CPC estimates.**
Every keyword must have a CPC estimate in USD. Use regional benchmarks where exact data is unavailable (e.g. Thailand CPC runs ~58% below US average per WordStream/ASEAN UP data). State the methodology.

**Rule 3: Real competitor verification.**
For every keyword in the top 20, Scriptor must verify the actual live SERP using Brave Search. Record who ranks at Position 1, 2, and 3. Do not guess. Do not use memory. Check live.

**Rule 4: Write in chunks, not one shot.**
Scriptor's write tool has a size limit. Every report must be written section by section, confirming each file saved before moving to the next. Never attempt to write an entire report in a single tool call. The sequence is: write → confirm saved → write next section → confirm saved → continue.

**Rule 5: Confidence levels must be stated.**
Every volume/CPC estimate must include a confidence band:
- High confidence (±15%): well-established keywords with strong SERP signals
- Medium confidence (±25%): established topics with some data
- Lower confidence (±40%): new/emerging terms with sparse results

## PART 3 — MANDATORY REPORT STRUCTURE

Every report must contain the following sections in this order. Sections may be expanded but never removed.

---

### Section 0 — Front Matter (MDX Frontmatter)

```mdx
---
title: "[Topic] Research Report"
description: "[One sentence summary of the report's key finding]"
date: "YYYY-MM-DD"
author: "Scriptor"
---
```

---

### Section 1 — Title & Executive Summary

**What it contains:**
- Report title as H1
- "Compiled by Scriptor · Research Date: [Month YYYY]" as bold subtitle
- 2-3 paragraph executive summary covering:
  - The market/topic context in one sentence
  - The single most important finding (lead with this)
  - The core opportunity or recommendation

**Tone:** Confident, direct. No hedging. State the finding, then prove it.

---

### Section 2 — Market Landscape / Competitive Overview

**What it contains:**
- 1-2 paragraphs of context
- **Infographic: Competitor Map SVG** (see Part 4 for spec)
- Prose analysis of the competitive landscape — what the infographic shows and why it matters
- Identification of the dominant player and their key weakness

---

### Section 3 — Deep Dive on the Dominant Competitor

**What it contains:**
- Detailed analysis of the #1 player in the space
- Their content structure, publishing patterns, SEO strengths
- Their specific weaknesses (E-E-A-T, schema, reputation, quality)
- Quantified reputation signals where available (Trustpilot score, Reddit sentiment, etc.)
- The specific gap their weakness creates

---

### Section 4 — Keyword Opportunity Analysis

**What it contains:**
- 1-2 paragraphs of context
- **Infographic: Keyword Priority Matrix SVG** (see Part 4 for spec)
- The highest-urgency keyword cluster called out explicitly with volumes
- Time-sensitivity note if applicable (policy changes, seasonal trends, etc.)

---

### Section 5 — Keyword Deep Dive

**What it contains:**
- Opening stat block: total monthly volume across verified keywords, average CPC, total ad value equivalent
- **Infographic: Top Keywords Bar Chart SVG** (see Part 4 for spec)
- Prose analysis of the SERP landscape pattern
- **Infographic: Who Owns the SERPs SVG** (see Part 4 for spec)
- "Where to Start" subsection — explicitly calling out the low-competition, high-volume keywords
- **Infographic: Act On These First SVG** (see Part 4 for spec)
- Prose closing the gap argument
- **Full Keyword Reference table** — all keywords organised by category with: Keyword | Est. Volume | Competition | Difficulty | Priority
- **Keywords We Can Win by Tier table:**
  - Tier 1: Win within 30-60 days (low competition, first-mover available)
  - Tier 2: Win in 60-90 days (medium competition, differentiation needed)
  - Tier 3: Long-term authority plays (90+ days, strong competition)
- Volume confidence methodology note

---

### Section 6 — Content Gaps

**What it contains:**
- The specific content gaps in the market — what exists, what is missing, why a new entrant wins
- Minimum 5 gaps, each with: what the current landscape offers, what is missing, what format wins
- Formats that perform in this niche (based on competitive observation)

---

### Section 7 — Competitive Positioning

**What it contains:**
- The recommended strategic positioning
- **Infographic: Positioning Matrix SVG** (see Part 4 for spec)
- The positioning statement
- What it means in practice (speed, credentials, UX, tools)

---

### Section 8 — Technical SEO Requirements

**What it contains:**
- Schema implementation priorities (P0/P1/P2)
- Recommended URL architecture
- Internal linking strategy
- E-E-A-T requirements specific to this niche

---

### Section 9 — Content Calendar

**What it contains:**
- **Infographic: Content Calendar SVG** (see Part 4 for spec)
- Month 1: Immediate priorities with specific article titles and target keywords
- Month 2: Core guides
- Months 3-6: Authority building content
- Months 6-12: Niche domination content
- Each entry: Article title | Target keyword | Est. volume | Content type

---

### Section 10 — Distribution & Monetisation

**What it contains:**
- Platform analysis (which platforms the competitors use, what the opportunity is)
- Recommended distribution strategy
- Monetisation model with specific revenue opportunities
- Affiliate opportunities with commission context where known

---

### Section 11 — Risk Factors

**What it contains:**
- Market risks (policy changes, competitor responses, algorithm updates)
- Technical risks (platform dependence, AI Overview impact)
- Mitigation strategy for each

---

### Section 12 — Conclusion

**What it contains:**
- The opportunity restated in one paragraph
- 5 success factors, numbered, bold
- Time-sensitivity note if applicable
- Closing line — confident, direct, imperial

---

### Section 13 — Appendix

**What it contains:**
- Research methodology
- Data sources
- Confidence levels and limitations
- Tools used (Brave Search API, web_fetch, competitive analysis)
- "Report compiled by Scriptor · OpenClaw Research · [Date]"

## PART 4 — INFOGRAPHIC SPECIFICATIONS

### The Design Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Every SVG infographic must follow these rules:

**Colors:**
- Background: `#0A0F1E` (deep navy — matches AppStarFamily site)
- Surface/card background: `#0F1628` or `#141B2D`
- Border/separator: `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)`
- Primary text: `#FFFFFF`
- Secondary text: `#8892A4`
- Gold accent: `#C9A84C` (used for titles, highlights, key callouts)
- Green (opportunity/win): `#10B981` or `#34D399`
- Amber (medium/caution): `#F59E0B`
- Red (high competition/avoid): `#EF4444`
- Cyan (official/government sources): `#38BDF8`
- Orange (commercial bias): `#FB923C`
- Purple (independent/data): `#A78BFA`

**Typography:**
- Font family in SVG: `font-family="system-ui, -apple-system, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif"`
- Titles: bold, gold (`#C9A84C`), letter-spacing
- Body: regular weight, white or light grey
- Labels: uppercase, small, tracked

**Branding:**
- Every SVG must include a footer line: `Scriptor / OpenClaw Research · [Month YYYY] · appstarfamily.com`
- No external image dependencies — all SVG must be self-contained

**Dimensions:**
- Standard landscape: `900×500` viewBox
- Data-heavy (many rows): `900×600` or `900×700`
- Card grids (2×3): `900×520`

**Style:**
- Rounded rectangles: `rx="8"` or `rx="12"`
- Subtle borders on cards: `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)`
- Column headers: uppercase, tracked, muted (`#4B5563`)
- Highlighted rows/columns: slightly lighter background
- Stat footers: gold text on dark background

### The 7 Standard Infographics (web reports) or 8 (app reports)

Every report includes these 7 SVGs. Topic-specific infographics may be added but these 7 are always present. App reports add a mandatory 8th SVG — see Part 4B.

---

#### SVG 1: Competitor Map
**Filename:** `[topic]-competitor-map.svg`
**Purpose:** Show the competitive landscape at a glance
**Format:** Table with rows per competitor
**Required columns:** Site | Type | Authority | Key Strength | Key Weakness
**Design notes:**
- Highlight the dominant competitor row (slightly lighter bg, gold left border)
- Add a reputation badge to any competitor with known trust issues (e.g. "2.3★ Trustpilot")
- Authority shown as: Very High / High / Medium / Low with colour coding

---

#### SVG 2: Keyword Priority Matrix
**Filename:** `[topic]-keyword-matrix.svg`
**Purpose:** Show which keywords to target first vs later
**Format:** 2×2 quadrant. X-axis: Search Volume (Low→High). Y-axis: Competition (Low→High, inverted so low competition is at top)
**Quadrants:**
- Top right (High Volume + Low Competition): **"ACT NOW — WINDOW CLOSING"** — green background, bold
- Top left (Low Volume + Low Competition): "Long Tail Targets" — subtle
- Bottom right (High Volume + High Competition): "High Competition — Build Authority First" — amber
- Bottom left (Low Volume + High Competition): "Avoid / Low ROI" — muted
**Keywords plotted as pill/chip labels in correct quadrant**
**Footer:** Urgency note about time-sensitive window if applicable

---

#### SVG 3: Top Keywords Bar Chart
**Filename:** `[topic]-top-keywords.svg`
**Purpose:** Show search volume and CPC for top 10 keywords
**Format:** Horizontal bar chart
**Each row shows:** Keyword name | Bar sized by volume | Volume number | CPC
**Color coding:** Green = low competition opportunity / Amber = medium / Red = high competition
**Footer stat bar:** Total volume · Total ad value · Average CPC · Confidence range

---

#### SVG 4: Who Owns the SERPs
**Filename:** `[topic]-serp-ownership.svg`
**Purpose:** Show who ranks for each keyword and where the gap is
**Format:** Table. Rows = top 10 keywords (with volume + CPC). Columns = Position 1 / Position 2 / Position 3 / THE GAP
**Color-coded dots per site type:**
- Cyan = Official/government (authoritative but dry)
- Orange = Commercial/legal (biased, lead-gen)
- Purple = Independent/data (neutral but limited)
- Grey = Other/niche
**Gap column:** Gold background, bold text, 2-line format (gap title + one-line explanation)
**Footer:** Source attribution + appstarfamily.com

---

#### SVG 5: Act On These First
**Filename:** `[topic]-act-now.svg`
**Purpose:** Make the immediate action obvious
**Format:** 2×3 grid of priority cards (6 keywords)
**Each card contains:**
- Rank badge (numbered 1-6, color-coded green/amber by tier)
- Keyword name (bold, gold)
- Volume/mo + CPC on one line
- "#1 now: [current ranking site]"
- One-line "why we win" rationale
**Header:** "Act On These First — Low Competition, High Volume"
**Subheader:** "Keywords combining meaningful volume with weak or fragmented competition · Publish these in Month 1"
**Footer:** The pattern statement in gold

---

#### SVG 6: Content Calendar
**Filename:** `[topic]-content-calendar.svg`
**Purpose:** Show the publishing roadmap visually
**Format:** 3-column grid (Month 1 / Month 2 / Months 3-6)
**Each column:** Column header + 3 content cards
**Each card:** Article title + target keyword + priority indicator (🔴 Immediate / 🟡 Soon / 🟢 Planned)
**Design:** Each column has a subtle left border in the column's urgency color

---

#### SVG 7: Positioning Matrix
**Filename:** `[topic]-positioning-matrix.svg`
**Purpose:** Show the strategic positioning gap
**Format:** 2×2 quadrant. X-axis: Speed (Slow→Fast). Y-axis: Credibility (Low→High)
**Plot existing competitors as labelled dots**
**Target position:** Gold star in the top-right quadrant, labelled "THE GAP" or "TARGET POSITION"
**The quadrant we own:** Top right — Fast + High Credibility — highlighted with subtle gold border

## PART 6 — MDX RULES (CRITICAL)

The AppStarFamily MDX renderer does **NOT** support inline HTML or SVG. Violating this rule causes literal text output instead of rendered content.

**NEVER do this:**
```mdx
<div class="card">...</div>
<svg>...</svg>
<table style="...">...</table>
```

**ALWAYS do this for infographics:**
```mdx
![Descriptive Title](/reports/infographics/[topic]-[name].svg)
```

SVGs are saved as static files in `public/reports/infographics/` and referenced as standard markdown images. This is the only correct approach.

**MDX prose rules:**
- Use standard markdown only: `##` headers, `**bold**`, `*italic*`, standard tables, standard lists
- Infographic images always have a descriptive alt text
- Italic caption under each image (the image filename concept, e.g. `*Top 10 Keywords by Monthly Search Volume*`)
- Sections separated by `---` horizontal rules
