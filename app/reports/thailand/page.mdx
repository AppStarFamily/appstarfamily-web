# Thailand Expat SEO Research Report

**Compiled by Scriptor · Research Date: March 2026**

The Thailand expat content market is experiencing significant volatility due to major visa policy changes in 2026. The competitive landscape is dominated by established players with varying content quality and authority levels.

**Key Finding:** The visa policy changes — 60→30 day reduction, DTV visa introduction, LTR visa updates — have created an information gap that new entrants can exploit with timely, authoritative content.

---

## The Competitive Landscape

Seven major players dominate this market. The Thaiger leads on volume and speed. Bangkok Post leads on credibility. Everyone else falls somewhere in between — and the gap for a fast, accurate, authoritative alternative is wide open.

![Thailand Expat Content Market — Competitor Map](/reports/infographics/thailand-competitor-map.svg)

The most striking finding from this analysis: **The Thaiger has a Trustpilot rating of 2.3/5 and Reddit's consensus is "avoid the Thaiger, it's pure crap."** They dominate through SEO volume and publishing speed, not quality. This is the opportunity.

---

## The Thaiger Deep Dive

The Thaiger's content operation is built around speed, not accuracy. Their guide structure is solid — direct answer intro, H2-based sections, "key takeaways" boxes — but the execution is inconsistent. Some articles are thorough. Many are thin rewrites of other sources.

**Their SEO weaknesses:**
- No visible schema markup (FAQ, HowTo, Article) — a significant gap
- No breadcrumb navigation
- No author bylines — an E-E-A-T liability
- Category pages are thin
- No publication dates on some content

**Their content moat is social amplification** (281K Facebook, 112K Instagram), not content quality. That moat can be breached with superior E-E-A-T signals.

## Content Gaps

Five clear gaps exist in the current market:

**1. Authoritative Visa Guides with E-E-A-T**
No competitor combines speed + credentials. Guides with lawyer or immigration consultant bylines, proper citations, and official source links would immediately stand out.

**2. Real-Time Policy Change Coverage**
The Thaiger is fast but often inaccurate. Bangkok Post is accurate but slow. The gap: fast + accurate + authoritative.

**3. Comparison Content**
Side-by-side visa comparisons and interactive decision tools barely exist. A "DTV vs LTR vs Retirement visa" comparison table would rank.

**4. Healthcare Content**
The Thaiger has hospital lists but no depth. Insurance guides, hospital cost breakdowns, and "which visa requires what insurance" content is almost entirely absent.

**5. Property Content**
The new 3M baht property visa is almost entirely uncovered. First-mover advantage is available here right now.

## Risk Factors

**Policy Volatility:** Thailand visa rules change frequently. Mitigate with dated content and a clear update schedule.

**Reputation:** The expat community is skeptical of new sources. One accuracy mistake causes lasting damage. Mitigate with conservative claims and official source citations from day one.

**Google AI Overviews:** May reduce click-through rates. Mitigate with FAQ schema (AI Overviews pull heavily from schema-marked FAQs) and email list building for direct traffic.
