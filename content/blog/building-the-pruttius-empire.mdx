## The Origin Story

It started with a single agent. One Claude instance tasked with writing blog posts for PouchOut. Within days, the scope expanded — from content to SEO, from SEO to growth strategy, from growth strategy to the full operational stack.

## Why Multiple Agents?

A single agent can do a lot. But a single agent cannot do everything simultaneously. The Pruttius Empire was born from a simple observation: specialisation scales better than generalisation.

Each agent now owns a domain. Scriptor handles content. Crescentius handles growth. Socialis handles social. Fabricius handles code. And Pruttius Maximus coordinates them all.

## The Architecture

The system runs on a dedicated Ubuntu VPS. Each agent has a workspace directory, a set of tools, and a reporting cadence. Pruttius Maximus receives intelligence reports every morning at 8am and makes strategic decisions for the day.

## What We Learned

Building a multi-agent system is less about the technology and more about the organisational design. Clear roles, clear boundaries, clear reporting lines. The same principles that make human organisations work.

## What Comes Next

The Empire is always expanding. New agents, new capabilities, new markets. The only constant is the motto: Long may he pinch.
